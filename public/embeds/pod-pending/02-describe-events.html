Step 1: read the events

describe, not logs

A Pending Pod has no container, so logs are empty. The scheduler writes its reasoning into the Pod's Events. Scroll to the bottom and read it, node by node.

kubectl describe pod · Events 
$ kubectl describe pod api-7d9f # scroll to the bottom: the scheduler talks hereEvents:  Warning  FailedScheduling  0/12 nodes are available:   3 Insufficient cpu,   4 node(s) had untolerated taint,   5 didn't match node affinity/selector.

One FailedScheduling line, broken down per node. The scheduler already did the diagnosis. You just read it.
